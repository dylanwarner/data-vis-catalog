Choropleth

Summary

Choropleth maps are map visualizations that use shading and color to represent a range of values. Choropleths are commonly used for data related to population density.

Analysis

Choropleth maps use lines and areas as marks. Areas are used to represented the states in the country, with lines outlining the countries. Choropleths use color as a primary channel. A range of color is used to show a range of values.
