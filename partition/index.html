Partition

Summary

Partition diagrams are another type of hierarchy chart similar to a treemap. Partitions are different in that they are adjacency diagrams. Nodes are either represented by arcs or rectangles and are drawn next to other nodes based on relations. Partitions are useful for hierarchical data or data that involves groupings.

Analysis

Partition diagrams use areas as marks. These areas are represented by either rectangles or arcs placed adjacent to one another. Size is used as a channel to represent a flow or hierarchy of information. The root parent at the top is the largest with a continual decrease in size as you move down layers of the chart. Color is another channel that can be used to group nodes or show depth.
